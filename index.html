Biography

I am a master student at University of Wisconsin Madison. I obtained my PhD degree in Statistics from Cornell Department of Statistics and Data Science, advised by Nathan Kallus and Madeleine Udell. Prior to my graduate school, I studied Mathematical Economics at Wuhan University.

Broadly, I work on data-driven decision making and causal inference, with a particular focus on developing statistical methods for problems arising in these areas. I am interested in developing reliable methodology for decision making and statistical inference when faced with practical data challenges such as measurement errors, omitted variables, missing data, among others.
